# Product

## Register

product

## Users

`rn-mt` is used by two overlapping groups.

The first group is React Native and Expo engineers who want to turn an existing
single-tenant app into a clean multi-tenant workspace without losing control of
their repo shape, native setup, or daily workflow.

The second group is contributors working inside the `rn-mt` monorepo itself.
They use the docs while implementing, debugging, reviewing, or verifying
changes. They are usually focused, moving quickly, and trying to answer one
precise question at a time.

## Product Purpose

`rn-mt` exists to make multitenancy conversion practical, explicit, and
repeatable for mobile teams. The docs site should help a developer get to the
right command, the right file, or the right workflow with as little friction as
possible.

Success means a developer can understand the product model, start using the
tool, debug a failure, or contribute a change without hunting through internal
planning material or guessing how the repo is wired.

## Brand Personality

- precise
- calm
- capable

The tone should feel like a serious engineering tool, not a startup landing
page. It should feel trustworthy, direct, and slightly opinionated.

## Anti-references

- glossy marketing docs with oversized hero treatments
- playful developer portals full of gradients, mascot energy, or novelty
- glass-heavy SaaS dashboards
- overly warm editorial sites that slow down scan speed
- documentation that hides practical commands behind promotional framing

## Design Principles

- Put the task first. The docs are a tool surface, not a brochure.
- Make scan paths obvious. A developer should know where to click and what to
  run within a few seconds.
- Prefer calm density over theatrical whitespace. The surface should feel
  deliberate and readable, not sparse for effect.
- Keep trust high. Familiar controls, consistent states, and visible structure
  matter more than decorative originality.
- Keep commands and ownership close to the explanation. The docs should reduce
  context-switching, not increase it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG AA contrast for all text and interactive states.
- Respect reduced motion preferences.
- Maintain visible focus states for keyboard users.
- Keep copy direct and jargon-aware, but not exclusionary.
- Preserve strong readability on narrow laptop screens as well as larger
  external monitors.
